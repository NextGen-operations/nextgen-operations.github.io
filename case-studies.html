Case Study 01 Workforce Management 24/7 Support Operations

Transforming a Complex Support Schedule and Reducing Overtime Costs

A client running a 24/7 support operation was locked into an inefficient shift model that was burning out staff, creating coverage gaps, and driving overtime costs well beyond acceptable levels. We redesigned their entire workforce operating model — delivering immediate, measurable impact.

✅ Outcome: 90% reduction in overtime hours and substantial annual cost savings
⚠️

The Challenge

The client operated a 24/7 support environment and was facing significant workforce management challenges due to a complicated and inefficient shift structure. Several interconnected issues had built up over time:

  • Excessive employee fatigue and reduced workforce flexibility
  • Limited managerial oversight during certain shift periods
  • No effective leave management controls or leave planning process
  • Frequent schedule gaps caused by unplanned absences
  • High dependency on overtime to maintain service coverage
  • Rising operational costs due to excessive overtime spending

As a result, overtime hours continued to climb while maintaining adequate support coverage became increasingly difficult — creating a cycle that was unsustainable for both the business and the team.

🔍

Our Approach

We began with a structured assessment phase before proposing any changes:

1

Stakeholder Conversations

Conducted discussions with team members, supervisors, and leadership to understand operational pain points and workforce challenges from all levels.

2

Scope Assessment

Reviewed support requirements, service coverage expectations, staffing levels, leave patterns, and workload distribution across the operation.

3

Leadership Engagement

Worked closely with senior management to align workforce planning recommendations with business objectives and service delivery expectations.

4

Workforce Planning Analysis

Evaluated existing schedules, leave utilization trends, shift coverage risks, and overtime patterns to identify root causes and opportunities for improvement.

🛠️

Solution Implemented

Based on our analysis, we redesigned the support operating model with a set of targeted changes:

  • Transitioned from the complicated shift structure to a standard, simplified 24/7 rotational support schedule
  • Strategically distributed team leads and senior resources across shifts to provide continuous oversight and decision-making support
  • Increased collaboration within shifts by assigning multiple team members together, improving workload sharing and knowledge transfer
  • Introduced improved leave planning and scheduling practices to proactively reduce coverage gaps
  • Created a more predictable staffing model that minimized the need for emergency overtime coverage
📊

Results

Key Outcomes

✅ 90% reduction in overtime hours
✅ Significant annual cost savings through reduced overtime expenditure
✅ Improved work-life balance for all support team members
✅ Better shift coverage and operational continuity
✅ Increased managerial visibility and oversight across all shifts
✅ Enhanced employee satisfaction and schedule flexibility
✅ More sustainable and predictable workforce planning model

Business Impact

The redesigned support schedule created a healthier and more efficient operating environment. Employees benefited from improved work-life balance, while leadership gained better control over workforce planning and operational costs.

The result was a more resilient 24/7 support operation with substantially lower overtime expenses, improved team performance, and a workforce model built to scale.
